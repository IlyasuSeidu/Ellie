# Hero Short

## Best Fit

- Primary platforms: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
- Recommended length: 25 to 45 seconds for TikTok/Reels, up to 60 seconds for Shorts
- Blend of angles: Micro-Drama + User Empathy
- Structure: hook in the first 1-2 seconds, visible proof by second 5, lesson before the close

## Hook Options

1. Nothing was broken. People were still hesitating.
2. I thought this was a visual tweak. It was really a trust problem.
3. The first minute of an app tells you whether it respects your time.

## Story-First Script

Today I was working on the pricing moment, the first impression, and the language experience.

On the surface those looked like separate jobs. They were really the same story.

The product was still asking people to think too hard in the first minute.

The clearest example was the pricing moment. Nothing was technically broken, but the pricing moment still made people hesitate.

The deeper lesson is this: people do not experience our work task by task. They experience whether the product feels obvious, trustworthy, and worth staying with.

So instead of adding more, I tried to make the meaning arrive faster.

Today tightened the pricing moment, the first impression, and the language experience. The language layer now reads across 1 languages. The pricing moment got the clearest pass: 1 languages tightened and the pricing moment now reads faster on first contact.

What is one tiny moment in a product that instantly makes you trust it less?

## Visual Beat Sheet

1. Open on the strongest before/after scene from the pricing moment.
2. Hard cut to the specific screen or flow affected by the pricing moment.
3. Overlay the tension line: "Nothing was technically broken, but the pricing moment still made people hesitate.".
4. Show one proof beat: updated copy, before/after sequence, or language spread.
5. Bring in the secondary moment: the reliability layer.
6. End on the question sticker or comment CTA.

## Edit Notes

- Keep the first on-screen text under 9 words.
- Use captions throughout.
- Open with the result or tension, not context.
- Cut every 1.5 to 2.5 seconds unless the before/after needs space.
- Show proof, not just face-to-camera commentary.
