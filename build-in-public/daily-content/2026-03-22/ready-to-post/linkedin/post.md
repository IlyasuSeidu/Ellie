# LinkedIn Ready Post

## Strategy

- Angle Mix: Founder Opinion + Mini Case Study
- Format: text post with one real product lesson
- Comment prompt: Where have you seen "localization complete" but user understanding still weak?

## Primary Post

Localization bugs are often product bugs in disguise.

Today I reviewed Ellie’s onboarding copy across three connected moments:

- pattern setup
- Aha preview
- paywall transition

The scheduling logic was already working.
The friction was narrative mismatch: each screen described value differently.

I rewrote the flow around one consistent promise:
Set your roster once, then preview upcoming shifts in clear calendar language.

No feature shipped.
No extra screens.
Just better product understanding at the exact moment people decide whether to continue.

For shift-work products, wording is not polish. It is usability.

Where have you seen "localization complete" but user understanding still weak?

## Builder Variation

A practical pattern that helped today:

1. Choose one product promise.
2. Audit every adjacent screen that should reinforce it.
3. Remove wording that sounds technically correct but behaviorally unclear.

In Ellie, that meant aligning setup, Aha, and paywall language around one sentence users can act on immediately.
