# Instagram Carousel

## Concept

Turn today's work into a seven-slide story that starts with tension, moves into the wrong assumption, and ends with a question people can answer from experience.

## Slides

1. **Cover**: The update that looked small but changed product trust
2. **The Problem**: People were still doing too much interpretation around the pricing moment, the first impression, and the language experience.
3. **The Wrong Assumption**: The tempting read was "this just needs polish." The deeper problem was that the meaning was arriving too slowly.
4. **The Turn**: The pricing moment forced a better decision.
5. **The Decision**: Strip friction out of the first moments and make the value legible faster.
6. **The Proof**: Today tightened the pricing moment, the first impression, and the language experience. The language layer now reads across 1 languages. The pricing moment got the clearest pass: 1 languages tightened and the pricing moment now reads faster on first contact.
7. **The Question**: Where does the experience still make a new person pause instead of move?

## Caption

Today I was working across the pricing moment, the first impression, and the language experience.

What tied them together was product clarity.

That is the kind of work I want to document more often: not just the shiny reveal, but the moment a product gets easier to trust.

The most useful part of the day was the pricing moment. Not because it was loud, but because it exposed a wrong assumption and replaced it with a clearer one.

If you build products, where do you most often see hidden trust leaks: onboarding, pricing, data, or something else?

## Visual Direction

- Use one strong cover line with a dark high-contrast background.
- Show one screenshot or code snippet only when it proves the point.
- Keep each slide to one idea and one emotional beat.
