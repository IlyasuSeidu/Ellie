# X Thread — January 26, 2026

**1/**
If the first real choice in your app feels like paperwork, people quit before the product earns a single point of trust.

That is the problem I was solving today on Ellie.

---

**2/**
Onboarding for shift workers has a specific trap.

Most workers do not know the official name of their roster pattern. They know what their weeks feel like — days on, days off, which phase they are in right now.

Ask them to name it first and they hesitate. Hesitation at step two is a product failure.

---

**3/**
The tension: I could add more explanation to the pattern step, or I could change how the choice is made.

More explanation is the easy answer. It is also usually the wrong one.

I rebuilt the shift pattern step as swipeable cards. Recognition instead of recall. The hardest decision in setup now takes a swipe, not a search.

---

**4/**
The same session, I reworked the introduction flow so it asks one thing at a time — conversational, not administrative. And I tightened the welcome screen so Ellie frames itself as a premium shift companion from the first moment, not a form to fill out.

Every one of those changes is the same decision: make the interaction match how the user already thinks.

---

**5/**
Builder lesson:

You do not make a hard decision easier by explaining it harder. You make it easier by changing how it feels.

Onboarding trust is not built in the copy. It is built in the motion, the sequence, and where you let people pause.
