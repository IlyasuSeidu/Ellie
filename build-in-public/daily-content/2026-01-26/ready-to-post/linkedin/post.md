# LinkedIn Ready Post

## Strategy

- Angle Mix: Founder Opinion + Mini Case Study
- Format: text post with one real product lesson
- Comment prompt: What onboarding step still feels like paperwork in most apps?

## Primary Post

Most onboarding fails before the product gets a fair chance.

Not because the questions are wrong. Because the experience of answering them feels like admin work.

That was the problem inside Ellie's setup flow. The right information was being collected. But asking someone to place themselves inside a repeating roster cycle, in one dense step, made the whole thing feel harder than it needed to be.

The fix was not better copy or a clearer label. It was changing the shape of the decision entirely. One question at a time. Swipeable cards for roster pattern selection instead of a list to read through. A welcome moment that signals premium from the first screen, not the third.

When the hardest decision in your setup flow feels lighter to move through, people stop hesitating and start trusting the product.

That is the real onboarding job. Not data collection. Trust transfer.

I am a miner building Ellie for the shift-work reality I actually live. I use AI-native, mostly vibe-coded workflows to move fast. The tooling is not the point. Turning real shift-work friction into defensible product decisions is.

What onboarding step still feels like paperwork in most apps?
