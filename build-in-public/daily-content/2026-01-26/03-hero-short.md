# Hero Short Pack

## Best Fit

- Primary platforms: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
- Recommended length: 25 to 45 seconds for TikTok and Reels, up to 60 seconds for Shorts
- Angle blend: Stakes / Consequence + Build Confession
- Structure: hook in the first 2 seconds, visible proof by second 5, lesson before the close

---

## Hook Options

1. If choosing your roster feels like paperwork, people quit before the app earns trust.
2. I rebuilt the pattern picker until it stopped feeling like a form.
3. Shift patterns are not interesting. The interaction has to be.

---

## Story-First Script

If choosing your roster feels like paperwork, people quit before the app earns trust.

That was the real problem.

The first part of the day was structural: a cleaner onboarding path, a stronger welcome screen, a country selector that made setup feel like one continuous move instead of scattered decisions.

The harder problem came after that.

Shift pattern selection was still asking people to read jargon when most workers just know what their weeks feel like. They know the rhythm. They do not know the label.

So I rebuilt that step as swipeable cards.

Not because swiping is a trend. Because the old interaction made a simple decision feel heavier than it needed to.

The lesson is not complicated: you do not make the pattern interesting. You make it easier to recognize.

What onboarding step in most apps still feels like paperwork to you?

---

## Visual Beat Sheet

1. Open on the swipeable pattern cards already in motion. No title card. No intro.
2. Hard cut to the hook line on screen: "If choosing your roster feels like paperwork, people quit before the app earns trust."
3. One tight before/after beat: static list selection versus the swipeable card version.
4. Brief flash of the welcome screen or introduction flow to show the larger path got cleaner too.
5. Close on the question as a sticker or pinned comment CTA.

---

## Caption Draft

Ellie's onboarding did not need more screens.

It needed to stop feeling like admin.

First the flow got structure: a cleaner path, a stronger welcome moment, country selection folded into setup so nothing felt out of place.

Then the hardest decision in the whole flow got rebuilt. Shift pattern selection stopped being a list and started being something a person could move through without reading the fine print.

The fix was not more explanation. It was a better interaction.

What onboarding step still feels like paperwork in most apps?

---

## Edit Notes

- Open on motion. Show the cards moving before any text appears.
- Keep the before/after proof beat tight: two cuts maximum.
- Captions on throughout. Mining shift workers watch on mute.
- End on a question people can answer from their own experience, not from knowing the product.
