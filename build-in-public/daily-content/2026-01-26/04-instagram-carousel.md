## Slide 2 — The Real Tension

Most shift workers do not know the official name of their roster pattern.

They know what their weeks feel like.

Days on. Days off. Rotate. Repeat.

A setup screen that asks for the official name is asking the wrong question.

---

## Slide 3 — The Wrong Assumption

The wrong assumption:

> "If we explain it better, people will figure it out."

More explanation is not the fix when the interaction itself is the friction.

---

## Slide 4 — What Changed

The shift pattern screen was rebuilt from a heavy list into swipeable cards.

One card. One pattern. Swipe until it matches your life.

The introduction flow was reworked to ask one thing at a time — conversational, not administrative.

The welcome screen was rebuilt to frame Ellie as a premium shift companion from the first moment.

---

## Slide 5 — The Proof

When the interaction is right, people do not need to read their way through setup.

They recognize their schedule.

They move forward.

That is the difference between onboarding that builds trust and onboarding that builds doubt.

---

## Slide 6 — The Principle

**You do not make a boring decision exciting by adding words.**

**You make it easier to move through.**

Onboarding earns trust in motion, not in explanation.
