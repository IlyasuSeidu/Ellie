# LinkedIn Ready Post

## Strategy

- Angle Mix: Founder Opinion + Mini Case Study
- Format: text post with one real product lesson
- Comment prompt: Where does localization still masquerade as a "copy-only" task in your product?

## Primary Post

Localization is a trust system, not a translation task.

Today in Ellie, we fixed onboarding and paywall friction that only showed up clearly in long-language layouts.

The copy itself was mostly correct. The product experience still felt unstable because key CTA lines wrapped at decision moments.

What we changed:

- tightened high-impact CTA copy across 11 locales
- hardened layout behavior in onboarding/paywall decision screens
- added tests where long-locale failures hide until real usage

Result: better first-minute confidence in the exact moments where users decide to continue or leave.

Where does localization still masquerade as a "copy-only" task in your product?

## Builder Variation

I build Ellie as a miner solving problems I see around shift workers.

Today’s lesson: if localization, layout, and motion are owned separately, trust breaks in the seams.

Treat them as one decision surface and your onboarding gets stronger fast.
