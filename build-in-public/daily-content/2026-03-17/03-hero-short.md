# Hero Short Pack

## Best Fit

- Primary platforms: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
- Recommended length: 25 to 45 seconds for TikTok and Reels, up to 60 seconds for Shorts
- Angle blend: Micro-Drama + Before-After
- Structure: hook in the first 2 seconds, visible proof by second 5, lesson before the close

---

## Hook Options

1. Nothing was broken. People were still hesitating.
2. I thought it was cosmetic. It was actually a trust problem.
3. The first minute of an app tells a shift worker whether it respects their time.

---

## Story-First Script

Nothing was technically broken.

But the first minute of Ellie was still making people pause.

The welcome screen, the pricing moment, the language experience — on the surface those looked like three separate jobs. They were really one problem: the product was asking people to think too hard before they had a reason to stay.

The clearest example was the language experience. Ellie supports 11 languages. But finishing that work only counts if it reads instantly in every one of them. So I went back through all 11 and removed the last edges that were still slowing comprehension.

Same logic applied to the pricing moment. Nothing was broken. But it was still making people hesitate. I tightened it until the meaning arrived faster.

That is the pattern I keep returning to. Not adding more. Removing the tiny pauses that make a product feel harder than it should.

What is one small moment in a product that makes you trust it less immediately?

---

## Visual Beat Sheet

1. Open on the strongest before-and-after from the language experience — one screen, two states.
2. Hard cut to the specific moment that changed: updated copy, cleaner layout, or language spread across 11 options.
3. Overlay the tension line on screen: _Nothing was broken. People were still hesitating._
4. Show the pricing moment: before state first, then the tightened version.
5. Pull back to the welcome screen as the connective thread — first impression, pricing, language, one story.
6. Close on the question sticker or comment CTA.

---

## Edit Notes

- Keep the first on-screen text under 9 words.
- Use captions throughout — mining environments and shift workers often watch without sound.
- Open on the result or the tension, never on context.
- Cut every 1.5 to 2.5 seconds unless the before-after needs a breath.
- Show the screen changing. Proof beats commentary every time.
