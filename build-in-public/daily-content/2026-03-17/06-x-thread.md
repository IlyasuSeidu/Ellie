# 06-x-thread.md

1/ The product worked. The first minute still made people hesitate.

That is a different problem than a bug. It is a trust problem.

---

2/ Three moments carry that trust: the welcome screen, the pricing moment, and the language experience.

None were broken. All were asking people to think harder than they should.

That is the tension nobody talks about. Working software and clear software are not the same thing.

---

3/ The decision was to stop treating polish as cosmetics.

Sharper meaning, not nicer styling. Every edit had one test: does the next action now feel inevitable?

That is the standard I held today across all three moments.

---

4/ The clearest proof is the language experience.

Ellie supports 11 languages. Finishing that layer only matters if every version reads instantly on first contact.

Today the last confusing edges came out. Eleven languages tightened. The experience now reads the way the product was always supposed to feel.

---

5/ The lesson I keep relearning:

Completion posts work when they show the last 10 percent that made the experience click, not the 90 percent that was already done.

The final pass is where trust is actually built or lost.

---

6/ Question for builders and operators:

Which moment in your product still asks people to think harder than they should?

Reply and tell me. I am genuinely curious where that friction lives for other people building in this space.
