# Hero Short Pack

## Best Fit

- Primary platforms: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
- Recommended length: 30 to 45 seconds for TikTok and Reels, up to 60 seconds for Shorts
- Angle blend: Stakes / Consequence + Build Confession
- Structure: hook in the first two seconds, proof by second six, lesson before the close

---

## Hook Options

1. If the schedule answer is wrong, the animation means nothing.
2. The first screen looked good. The question was whether it was standing on anything real.
3. Day one had two jobs. Only one of them was visible.

---

## Story-First Script

If the schedule answer is wrong, the animation means nothing.

That was the first decision building Ellie.

Before any onboarding screen, before the gold theme and the haptic feedback, there had to be a foundation that shift workers could actually trust.

Shift math. Date logic. The reliability layer underneath the answer a miner checks before a night shift.

Once that was solid, the visible layer followed. The onboarding screen. The progress header that makes setup feel finite. The inputs and cards sitting on top of groundwork that had already been tested.

The surface is what people see first. But the foundation is what decides whether they come back.

I am a miner building this. I know what it costs when the answer feels wrong at 4 a.m.

So trustworthy came before impressive. Every time.

What is one product moment where the design means nothing if the underlying answer is shaky?

---

## Visual Beat Sheet

1. Open on the strongest onboarding screen. Gold theme, clean motion, premium feel.
2. Cut immediately to the hook line: "If the schedule answer is wrong, the animation means nothing."
3. Flash one fast proof beat showing the reliability layer underneath. Keep it visual and quick.
4. Return to the visible onboarding flow so the audience sees what is now sitting on top of that foundation.
5. Close face-to-camera or text on screen with the question.

---

## Edit Notes

- Open on the visible layer first. Let the audience see the premium surface.
- Cut fast to the tension line. Do not explain before you create stakes.
- Keep proof beats visual and brief. One or two seconds each.
- Use captions throughout. Most short-form is watched without sound.
- End with the question. It works for builders and for shift workers. Both have lived this.
