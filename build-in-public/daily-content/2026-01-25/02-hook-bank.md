# Hook Bank

Use these as opening lines for Reels, TikTok, Shorts, LinkedIn, X, or Threads. Every hook carries tension, proof, or a reaction trigger.

1. If "Tomorrow: Night Shift" is wrong, the animation does not matter.
2. Day 1 had two jobs: build the answer, then build the first screen people could trust.
3. Most day-1 posts chase something visible. I needed something believable.
4. By the end of day 1 there was a welcome screen. The harder part was making sure it stood on real shift logic.
5. The first screen is easy to show. The underneath truth is harder to earn.
6. Trust is not a feature you add after the interface looks good.
7. The welcome screen was never the real risk. The answer behind it was.
8. If the first visible layer sits on shaky logic, design just hides the problem for a few seconds.
9. The product did not start with animations. It started with whether tomorrow could be trusted.
10. Day 1 was not setup. It was the first decision about whether Ellie would deserve belief.
11. What do you secure first when the user cannot afford a wrong answer?
12. The more interesting day-1 story is not what appeared on screen. It is what made that screen safe to believe.
